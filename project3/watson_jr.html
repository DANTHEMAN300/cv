Thomas J. Watson, Jr.

Thomas John Watson, Jr. was the son of Thomas J. Watson, Sr. and his successor at IBM. Watson Jr. was born in Dayton, Ohio in 1914. After obtaining a B.A. degree from Brown University in 1937, he began working at IBM as a salesman. In 1952 he was elected president of IBM after his father stepped down, and four years later, a month before his father died, he became CEO of the company, and then chairman in 1961 (Thomas J Watson, Jr., n.d.).

The passing of the baton between Watsons marked a major change in IBM. Under Watson Sr., IBM had been a successful albeit medium-sized company that sold products such as mechanical tabulators and typewriters. Under Watson, Jr., the company transitioned into selling computers and quickly became a world leader in that market. By the time Watson Jr. stepped down as chairman and CEO in 1971, IBM's staff had increased from 72,500 worldwide to over 270,000, and its net income had increased from $87 million to $1.079 billion. Fortune Magazine dubbed Watson Jr. the "most successful capitalist who ever lived". On December 31, 1993, Watson Jr. died in Greenwich, New York due to complications with a stroke. He was 79 (Thomas J. Watson, Jr., n.d.).
